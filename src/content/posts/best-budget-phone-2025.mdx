## How to choose: gaming vs camera vs daily use

### Gaming

<div className="justify">
Look for a **Snapdragon 7+ Gen 3 or Dimensity 8300 Ultra** minimum. These chips have dedicated gaming GPU cores that handle sustained loads without throttling. A high refresh rate (120Hz or 144Hz) and a large battery (5000mAh+) matter more than camera megapixels. Fast charging is a bonus — gaming drains batteries fast.
</div>

**Top picks:** POCO X6 Pro · POCO F6 · realme GT 7T

### Camera / video

<div className="justify">
Megapixels alone are misleading. Look for **OIS (Optical Image Stabilisation)**, a large sensor size (Sony IMX or Samsung ISOCELL series), and at least one capable ultra-wide. For video, check if it records 4K at 30fps. The Leica and Sony partnerships on Xiaomi phones punch well above their price.
</div>

**Top picks:** Xiaomi 15T · Redmi Note 13 Pro+ · Infinix Zero 40 5G

### Daily use

<div className="justify">
Prioritize **software longevity** (Samsung gives 4 OS updates), a clean UI, and solid battery life. IP ratings for water resistance are a bonus. You do not need the fastest chip — the Exynos 1480 and Dimensity 7200 handle everything outside of heavy gaming just fine.
</div>

**Top picks:** Samsung Galaxy A56 5G · Samsung Galaxy A36 5G · realme GT 7T

## Things to always check before buying

- **RAM** — Minimum 8GB in 2025. 6GB will slow down noticeably with a few apps open.
- **Storage** — 128GB fills up fast. Go for 256GB if you shoot lots of photos or videos.
- **Charging speed** — 67W+ gets you a full charge in under an hour. Anything below 33W feels slow now.
- **Software updates** — Samsung leads with 4 years. Xiaomi and realme offer 2–3 years. Check before committing.
- **Band support** — If you use a non-Maxis/Celcom carrier, verify that 5G Sub-6GHz bands are supported for your network.
