## How to choose the right flagship

<div className="justify">
### Best for gaming

The **ASUS ROG Phone 9 Pro** is in a category of its own — no other phone ships with a dedicated cooling accessory, pressure-sensitive side triggers, and a 185Hz display tuned for competitive play. If you play casually but still want flagship performance, the **OnePlus 13** and **OPPO Find X8 Pro** both run Snapdragon 8 Elite or Dimensity 9400 with 5900–6000mAh batteries that last long sessions.

### Best for camera and video

The answer depends on what you shoot. For **video**, nothing beats the **iPhone 16 Pro Max** — 4K 120fps Dolby Vision is a full generation ahead of Android at this price. For **still photography**, the **Xiaomi 15 Ultra** with its 1-inch Leica sensor produces the largest and most detailed photos. For **zoom**, the **vivo X200 Pro** with a 200MP periscope telephoto is untouchable. For **effortless AI-enhanced shots**, the **Google Pixel 9 Pro XL** consistently produces stunning results with minimal effort.

### Best for daily use

The **Samsung Galaxy S25+** is the sweet spot — slim, Snapdragon 8 Elite fast, and backed by a 7-year update promise. If you are in the Apple ecosystem, the **iPhone 16 Pro Max** is the natural choice. If software longevity matters most, both the **Pixel 9 Pro XL** and **S25 Ultra** now guarantee seven years of updates.
</div>

## What actually separates a flagship from a mid-ranger

<div className="justify">
- **Sustained performance** — A flagship chip runs at full speed for 30 minutes of gaming. Mid-range chips throttle down to 60–70% to manage heat.
- **Sensor size** — A 1/1.3-inch or larger sensor captures 3–5× more light than the 1/2.5-inch sensors in budget phones. The difference is visible in dim restaurants and concerts.
- **Optical zoom** — True periscope telephoto lenses at 3x, 5x, or 10x are exclusive to flagships. Digital zoom on a budget phone is just a crop.
- **Build quality** — IP68 rating, Gorilla Glass Victus, and titanium or aerospace aluminium frames are not found below RM 3,000.
- **Software lifespan** — Samsung and Google now promise 7 years of updates. A flagship bought today will be supported until 2031–2032.
</div>
